What is Doping?

In the world of sports, doping refers to the use of banned substances or methods by athletes to improve their performance artificially.This is considered cheating and can have serious health consequences for athletes.

Banned Substances: These include performance-enhancing drugs (PEDs) like steroids, stimulants, and diuretics. These substances can increase muscle mass, boost endurance, and mask fatigue, giving athletes an unfair edge.

Prohibited Methods: This goes beyond just substances. Techniques like blood doping (increasing red blood cells to carry more oxygen) and gene doping (altering genes for athletic traits) are also banned.

Why it's a Problem: Doping undermines the fair play principle in sports. It creates an uneven playing field where athletes who dope have an unfair advantage over those who don't. Additionally, many performance-enhancing drugs have serious health risks.

Regulation: The World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) is responsible for regulating doping in sports. They maintain a list of banned substances and methods, and athletes are subject to testing both in and out of competition.
