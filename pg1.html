SUN YANG

Sun Yang, China's swimming champion, was a giant in the pool. But in 2018, a storm brewed outside the water. Doping testers showed up unannounced at his home for a blood and urine sample. Things got tense. Sun questioned their credentials, and the situation escalated. In the end, a security guard, following Sun's orders, smashed a vial of his blood with a hammer, making it unusable for testing.

This was a major violation. Swimming's governing body, FINA, initially gave Sun a slap on the wrist. But the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) cried foul. They took the case to the Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS), a high court for sporting disputes. The world watched as the dramatic hearing, livestreamed for the first time, unfolded.

Sun argued the testers weren't legitimate. CAS disagreed. They ruled that Sun tampering with the sample was a clear violation. Doping is a serious offense, and since this was his second offense (he'd been caught earlier), they threw the book at him. Sun received an eight-year ban, effectively ending his swimming career.

Sun wouldn't go down without a fight. He appealed to the Swiss Federal Tribunal, Switzerland's highest court. He claimed a bias against him by one of the judges. But the court upheld the CAS decision. Sun's swimming days were over.

The Sun Yang case was a murky one. Was he a hero fighting against corrupt testers, or a cheat trying to wiggle out of a positive test? The world may never know for sure. But one thing was clear: tampering with a doping test carries a heavy price.
